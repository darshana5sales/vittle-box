Frequently Asked

The questions every restaurant owner asks us.

Will this affect my existing restaurant brand and customers?
+
Not at all. Our brands operate purely as delivery-only listings on Swiggy and Zomato. Your dine-in customers will never know — the only thing that changes is that your kitchen runs more orders during the hours you choose.
What does my kitchen need to qualify?
+
A minimum of 180 sq ft of kitchen space, a valid FSSAI license, at least one chef available for our brand hours, and basic equipment suited to the cuisines you choose. We conduct a 20-minute site audit before onboarding.
Who pays for raw materials and packaging?
+
You do — but we supply packaging at partner rates (typically 40% below market) and share verified vendor lists for raw materials. All of this is already accounted for in the 78% net revenue share you receive.
What if my food quality doesn't match your standards?
+
That's exactly why we train your team for 3–5 days before going live. We also run weekly QC audits in the first month. If quality drops, we work with you to fix it. If it doesn't recover, either side can exit with 30 days notice.
How fast do payments come in?
+
Every Monday, for orders completed the previous week. You'll see a live dashboard with every single order, customer rating, and rupee accounted for. No 30-day waits. No reconciliation headaches.
Can I start with just one brand to test?
+
Absolutely. Most of our partners start with 1–2 brands and scale to 4–6 within 90 days once they see the numbers. We'll recommend which brand fits your kitchen best during the site audit.
